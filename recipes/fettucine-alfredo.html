Fettucine Alfredo

A classic Italian-American dish.

Fettucine Alfredo is a classic dish that goes well with most Italian food or it can also be enjoyed on its own. This dish is cheesy and delicious with a rich alfredo sauce that's made from scratch.

It is also very easy to make and can be done in under 20 minutes!

Ingredients

  • 14 oz Fettucine
  • 5 cloves garlic, finely minced or grated
  • 2 cups grated parmigiano reggiano cheese
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter
  • 1 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tsp chopped thyme
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Place fettucine in a pot of boiling water that has been generously seasoned with salt. Cook the pasta until it's al dente.
  2. Add butter and heavy cream in a large cold pan. Set to medium heat and constantly stir the butter and heavy cream together until the butter is melted.
  3. Increase the heat slightly and bring to a light simmer. Simmer, stirring occasionally for 4 minutes or until lightly thickened.
  4. Remove from heat and add the chopped thyme and minced garlic. Slowly add the parmigiano reggiano cheese, stirring quickly to avoid lumps and throughly combine eveything. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Set aside 1 cup of pasta water. Drain the pasta.
  6. Add pasta to alfredo sauce. Toss the sauce and pasta together using pasta water to throughly emulsify.
